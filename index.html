iTinerant

Travel App Concept

CHALLENGE

I started by conducting surveys asking how people travel. I probed for pain points to solve with an app. I also researched similar apps so we can add a unique feature. User personas and user journey maps were created to design user flow diagrams. And finally, I sketched out wireframes and built clickable prototypes.

SOLUTION

Based on the findings, the four features incorporated into the design were a translator, itinerary log, currency exchange converter, and a way to find places to go / things to do. Other apps currently offer those features, so after further surveys and interviews, I decided to add a feature to help prevent leaving important things behind.

Pill Bug

Medication Scheduler Prototype

CHALLENGE

We had to create an app by interviewing people and finding a pain point in their daily routine. In my interviews, the topic of medications came up, and the challenges of keeping track of the dosages. Some are taken on different days, at different times of the day. Some are taken with food, others on an empty stomach. Another concern was cross-contamination with other meds, supplements, and food.

SOLUTION

We decided to go with a user persona who is a senior citizen and through the user journey map we realized we needed to design a straight forward, easy to read and use app. I decided the 2 main features would be a scanner to upload all the medication data and autopopulate the scheduler calendar and also a drug interaction checker. We did several A/B tests before settling on our high-fidelity clickable prototypes.

NASA

Information Architecture
Redesign

CHALLENGE

The NASA website has a wealth of information. Through user reviews we found that the main reason people visit the site is to view the galleries. After reviewing the homepage we found that the presentation of the articles was very random. We wanted to simplify the site, so that it is easier for viewers to digest the large amount of information.

SOLUTION

We started out by creating a sitemap of the current website up to the tertiary menu options. By card sorting we restructured the information architecture. We decided to highlight the next upcoming event on the homepage with an eye catching hero image. We condensed the navigation bar to just four links, with sub-menus and a visual breadcrumb trail.

iRecycle

Responsive Web Design
& Gamification

CHALLENGE

The website was very outdated and the style was plain. We first needed to understand the stakeholders' goals, which is to increase community involvement in recycling. We also looked at key performance indicators to track. The redesign should also be responsive as the users have access and use all platforms and devices.

SOLUTION

We decided that gamifying the website with opportunity to earn rewards and recognition by reaching goals, can incite competition and increase participation, which consequently will drive more traffic to the website. I also coded the website and made it responsive for mobile, tablet, and desktop.
